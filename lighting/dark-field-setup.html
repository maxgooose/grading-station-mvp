Key dimensions for the prototype

  • LED angle above screen: 10°–20°. Below 10° the LEDs themselves appear in frame. Above 20° the specular bounce starts hitting the camera.
  • LED height: just above screen plane — roughly at the phone's edge height, not a foot up.
  • LED distance from device edge: 3–6 cm. Closer = brighter scatter but more geometry sensitivity; farther = more forgiving but dimmer.
  • Camera distance: whatever your focal length requires for the whole device to fit. Typically 20–40 cm for a phone.
  • Base plate: matte black paper, foam board, or paint. Cannot be glossy.
  • Walls: black cloth / felt / painted cardboard, as light-tight as you can make it. Ambient room light is your enemy.

Scaling up from 2 lights to 4

  • 2 lights on opposite sides (e.g., N+S only): ~50–60% of scratches lit properly; parallel scratches get missed.
  • 2 lights on perpendicular axes (e.g., N+E): ~70–80%. Blind spots on the two diagonals.
  • 4 lights, all sides: ~95%+. Recommended.
  • Ring light at grazing angle (LED circle around device): essentially no orientation blind spots — industrial inspection standard.

How to find the right angle

  • Put the phone down, tape one LED strip on a side, room dark.
  • Tilt the strip up and down by hand. At one specific angle, scratches will suddenly glow white on a now-black screen. That's your lock angle.
  • Once you've found it, measure it (just eyeball it, ~12–15° is typical) and mount a rigid bracket at that angle for all 4 strips.
