P1 — FLOOR

24" × 12" plank — laid flat, no cuts
Stock: the 24"×12" board · Cuts: none · Reference marks only

No cuts The 24"×12" plank is your floor. Don't cut it — just draw the reference lines onto it so you know exactly where the 4 walls will glue/screw down.

  1. Find the midpoint of the long edge — measure 12" from one short edge. Mark across the full width: this is the dividing line between the cube footprint and the loading shelf.
  2. Pick which 12"×12" half is the cube footprint. Mark BACK, FRONT arrows on the plank so you don't get disoriented. (Front = where the shelf points / where the operator stands.)
  3. Draw the wall landing lines — the 4 edges of the 12"×12" cube footprint. The walls glue/screw down along these lines. Use a square to keep them at 90°.
  4. Done. Set this plank aside.

P2 — ROOF

12" × 12" — lens pass-through hole + bracket footprint on top
Stock: one 12"×12" square · Cuts: 1× ⌀2" lens hole (locked — measured 1.9" lens + 0.1" clearance) + bracket fixing screws (TBD per bracket) · Light-tightness strategy: camera bracket sits on TOP of panel, lens points DOWN through hole. Bracket base covers/seals the hole from above.

Mount strategy Camera bracket sits on TOP of P2; lens hangs DOWN through a ⌀2" hole into the box. Light-tightness comes from the bracket base footprint being larger than the hole + foam gasket beneath.

Cut 1× ⌀2" lens pass-through hole (placeholder — confirm by measuring) · Drill bracket fixing screws (count + spacing TBD per your bracket)

  1. Measure your camera + bracket first. Three things to write down before drilling:
    • Lens body widest diameter (across the zoom-ring area) → defines the hole size. Add 3mm slack.
    • Bracket base footprint (L × W). Must be wider than the hole on all sides.
    • Bracket mounting holes — does the base have screw holes for fixing to the panel? Count them, measure spacing.
  2. Find center. Draw the two diagonals corner-to-corner — they intersect at (6", 6"). Mark with a red dot.
  3. Mark the lens hole. Set a compass to (lens widest / 2 + 1.5mm). Default placeholder = ⌀2" (50mm).
  4. Mark the bracket footprint as a pencil rectangle centered on the hole. Confirm it covers the hole entirely with at least 5mm overlap on every side.
  5. Mark the bracket fixing screws at the bracket's measured screw spacing. Drill pilots matching your screw thread (typically M3 wood screws).
  6. Mark FRONT edge on the panel with an arrow (so the bracket points correctly when mounted).
  7. Cut the ⌀2" hole (use a 50mm hole saw or jigsaw + sanding). Drill the bracket pilot holes.
  8. Apply a black foam-tape ring around the hole on top of the panel BEFORE bolting the bracket down — when compressed, this seals any leak between the bracket base and the panel.

Why this works: the bracket body sits directly on the panel, completely covering the lens hole from above. The lens passes down through the hole, and the bracket's metal base + a foam gasket beneath it stops light from sneaking in around the lens barrel. Focus + zoom are adjusted by reaching in through the front door during setup, then locked with the lens's lock screws.

P3 — FRONT WALL

12" × 12" — door hatch + hinge/latch marks
Stock: one 12"×12" square · Cuts: 8" × 9" door hatch · Drill: hinge & latch pilots

Mimic the 4 red corner dots onto your plank using the arrows from each edge.

  1. Mark TOP and BOTTOM edges with marker.
  2. Use the 4 arrows as a recipe — each one says how far in from one edge to mark a dot:
    • From TOP edge, 1.5" down → 2 dots (one on each side)
    • From BOTTOM edge, 1.5" up → 2 dots
    • From LEFT edge, 2" in → 2 dots
    • From RIGHT edge, 2" in → 2 dots
    Where the perpendicular ticks intersect = the 4 corner dots of the door.
  3. Connect the 4 corner dots with a ruler.
  4. Mark the 3 hinge dots on the right line of the door (1" from top corner, mid, 1" from bottom corner) and the 1 latch dot on the left line at mid-height.
  5. Cut along the 4 connected lines. Drill the small dots.

P4 — BACK WALL

12" × 12" — wire pass-through hole (small)
Stock: one 12"×12" square · Cuts: 1× ⌀1" wire pass-through near bottom edge

Cut 1× ⌀1" hole at the bottom-center for LED + sensor wires running down into the utility box.

  1. Mark TOP and BOTTOM on the panel.
  2. Find the hole center: 6" from the left edge (= center horizontally), 1.5" up from the bottom edge.
  3. Mark the circle: 0.5" radius around that center point (= ⌀1" hole).
  4. Cut the hole. After assembly you'll seal it light-tight with foam-tape gasket around the wire bundle.

P5 — LEFT WALL

12" × 12" — no cuts now; mark future feed-in slot only
Stock: one 12"×12" square · Cuts now: none · Pencil-only: future 4"×4" feed-in slot (don't cut yet)

Do not cut Just pencil the rectangle so when you're ready to add a feeding mechanism, the cut location is already planned.

  1. Mark TOP/BOTTOM and FRONT/BACK edges on the panel — orientation matters.
  2. Mark a 4"×4" rectangle centered vertically (8" zone, 2" margin top & bottom), biased toward the FRONT edge so the feeding mech enters near where the operator stands. From the FRONT edge: 1" in to the near side of the rectangle; the rectangle extends 4" further toward the back.
  3. Draw it as a dashed light-pencil rectangle — visible enough to find later, light enough that you remember not to cut it now.

P6 — RIGHT WALL

12" × 12" — no cuts now; mark future cradle handle exit only
Stock: one 12"×12" square · Cuts now: none · Pencil-only: future ⌀1.5" cradle handle exit (don't cut yet)

Do not cut Just pencil the circle so the v2 U-cradle handle has a planned exit point.

  1. Mark TOP/BOTTOM and FRONT/BACK edges on the panel.
  2. Mark the circle center at exactly (6", 6") — the geometric center of the panel.
  3. Draw a light-pencil circle at 0.75" radius (= ⌀1.5" hole). Lightly enough that it reads "future" and not "cut me now."

ASSEMBLY ORDER (after cutting)

  1. Lay P1 (floor) flat — the reference lines you drew on it are your alignment guides.
  2. Glue/screw the 4 walls onto P1, standing them up along the cube-footprint reference lines:
    • P4 (back) first — sets the rear plane
    • P5 (left) and P6 (right) butted against P4's left/right ends
    • P3 (front) last — closes the cube
  3. Glue/screw P2 (roof) on top, with the FRONT arrow pointing toward P3 (the door wall).
  4. Reinstall the door piece (cut out from P3) into its opening with the 3 hinges on the right and the latch on the left.
  5. Tape every internal seam with matte-black duct tape (light-tightness).
  6. Add black felt gasket strip around the inside of the door opening.
  7. Run wires from the LED strips and any sensors through P4's wire pass-through, down into the utility box that the floor sits on.
