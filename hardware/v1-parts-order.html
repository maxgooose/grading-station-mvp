How the lights are controlled

  • Each light is plug-and-play — its own power plug + dimmer knob. No relay, no 12V adapter, no fuse, no wiring.
  • You switch bright-field ↔ dark-field by hand between the two shots — you're already opening the door to flip the phone, so it costs nothing.
  • Optional later: a ~$10 USB relay lets the PC switch them automatically. It drops in with zero rework when you want it.

Build notes

  • Inside of the box must be matte black everywhere — mandatory for dark-field contrast.
  • Bright-field panel = self-diffused, so no separate diffuser. Dark-field strip stays bare, on small black foam blocks to set the grazing angle.
  • If a light flickers in captures, nudge its dimmer off the lowest settings (cheap PWM dimmers can flicker at low brightness).
  • Cameras: plug the hub into a rear PC port; grab one camera at a time (5 cameras share USB 2.0 bandwidth).
  • Door (thin wood): pre-drill, short screws, and black-out any shiny hinge/knob.
